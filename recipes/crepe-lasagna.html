Crepe Lasagna

Description

Lasagna. I love lasagna as much as the next American, but if you ask me about the one thing I don't love, it's when the edges of the pasta get a little too dry and turn back into pasta-leather, y'know? Crepe lasagna solves that: instead of pasta that gets hard when it dries out, you've got crepes. They're much more able to survive a bit of extra toasting in the oven.

Only problem then: what are crepes? To be honest, I don't really know! I'm the STEM person and rely—heavily rely—on a Franco-American boyfriend to take care of things like that.

Otherwise, this dish is reasonably easy. It's a slow-cooking style, so you do a little bit of work then you can go do something else for a while while it sits and smells amazing.

Ingredients

  • 8-12 crepes in the fridge
    (the kind your boyfriend or roommate makes too many of)
  • 150g of dry mozzarella, or more to taste, shredded
  • ragù alla bolognese or make a batch from
    • 1 medium red onion, or yellow, chopped
    • 150g of celery, chopped
    • 400g of carrot, chopped
    • 150g of tomato paste
    • 400g of peeled tomatoes
    • 1L of smooth tomato puree
    • 300g of beef, ground
    • 200g of veal, ground
    • salt & pepper
    • olive oil
    • red table wine
    • whole milk

Steps

  1. If you have the ragù ready, skip to step 14, otherwise select a large stock pot, 12 or 16 liters is about right.
  2. Coat the bottom of the pot with a generous pour of olive oil and gently preheat.
  3. Add chopped onion and cook a few minutes until golden.
  4. Add celery and carrot. Continue to cook at a medium-high heat to develop the flavor of these aromatics, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add about 100 mL of wine. Evaporate off the alcohol.
  6. Add meat.
  7. As the meat browns, stir it to break it apart.
  8. Allow the juices from the meat to reduce—you want the water to evaporate before adding more liquid.
  9. Add another 100 mL of wine and evaporate it.
  10. Add tomato products. Wash remaining tomato from the containers with a bit of extra water.
  11. The key to any tomato sauce is patience, allowing it to cook for most of a day. Three hours is a minimum, but longer is better. Reduce heat to a light simmer, cover and occasionally check if more water is needed.
  12. Add salt and pepper to taste at some point.
  13. Allow the ragù to cool before continuing. It can be warm or chilled but you don't want to burn yourself during assembly.
  14. Select a lasagna pan, about 25x30 cm or 10x12 inches. Preheat oven to 175C or 350F.
  15. Start with a thin layer of ragù, then layer crepe, ragù, mozzarella. Repeat layers until the pan is filled.
    • If the crepes are an awkward size for the pan, feel free to cut and overlap them. They will all melt together once baked.
    • Keep the sauce and cheese layers thin - thinner than you would need in a pasta lasagna,
  16. Bake for about 40-60 minutes until cheese bubbles and sauce starts to simmer.
